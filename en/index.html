FAQ

Practical questions

What exactly do I submit when ordering? +

You submit the website URL, billing details and a short note about what you want the audit to focus on. If the context is advertising, redesign, weak enquiries or an agency input, add that to the note. It helps focus the output on the actual decision you need to make.

Is the audit automatic or human-reviewed? +

The base is created by an analytical system: signal collection, technical checks, custom scoring, UX heuristics and interpretation. Every report is reviewed personally before delivery — so the output has clear priorities and makes practical sense.

Why is the current price lower? +

The methodology, report format and parts of the system are being refined through real audits and early client feedback. The founder price reflects that — the output is usable now while the system keeps improving.

When does the audit make the most sense? +

It makes the most sense before investing in advertising, before a redesign, when the website gets too few enquiries, or when the offer is good but visitors do not understand it clearly enough. The audit does not add another marketing layer blindly; it first shows where the website may be losing clarity, trust or performance.

What if the website has good technical scores but still does not work commercially? +

That is exactly where SIGEL is useful. Technical health is only one layer. A website can be fast, indexable and formally correct while still failing to explain the offer, build trust, guide visitors to action or speak to the right customer type.

What do brand and customer archetypes mean? +

The brand archetype describes how the company comes across on the website: expert, creator, guide, innovator or another type of brand role. Customer archetypes help define the motivations, doubts and proof different visitor types need in order to understand and trust the offer.

Can agencies use the output? +

Yes. An agency can use the report as diagnostic input before consulting, redesign, SEO work or content strategy. By agreement, it can serve as internal preparation, client-facing audit input or the basis for the agency's own recommendation.

What does the audit not guarantee? +

The audit does not guarantee revenue growth, better search rankings or advertising campaign results. It is a diagnostic input for decision-making: it shows risks, barriers and priorities worth addressing before sending more budget into the website, advertising or redesign.
