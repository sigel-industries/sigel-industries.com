Too few enquiries

When the website exists, but does not bring enough contacts.

The audit distinguishes whether the problem lies in unclear offer, weak CTA, lack of trust, poor mobile path or the fact that the visitor does not know why to continue.

  • finds places where the decision breaks down,
  • names obstacles in the main user path,
  • recommends priorities for improving clarity and trust.
Weak marketing results

Before increasing the budget, check the website.

If advertising brings people to a website that does not explain value, does not build trust or does not guide them to the next step, performance is lost before contact.

  • the audit shows whether the website can persuade visitors,
  • reveals weak spots in communication and UX,
  • helps determine what to fix before the next campaign.
Redesign

So the new website is not just a prettier version of the same problem.

The audit helps create a redesign brief based on reality: what does not work, what to keep, what to change and what communication role the new website should take.

  • prioritizes content, structure, CTA and trust,
  • names the brand archetype and tone of communication,
  • helps give the agency or designer a clearer brief.
Unclear offer

A good offer is not enough if the website cannot say it.

Sigel helps clarify what the company really offers, whom it is for, why the visitor should trust it and how to adjust the message for different customer archetypes.

  • reveals generic or blurred formulations,
  • suggests clearer argumentation and proof elements,
  • connects the offer with customer motivation.
Stagnation

When the company has grown, but the website stayed in the old version.

The audit checks whether the website still matches the current offer, audience, price level, trust and the way the company wants to act on the market.

  • shows the mismatch between company reality and website,
  • names communication and strategic weaknesses,
  • gives priorities for what to change first.
