Market problem

Most companies do not start marketing badly. They simply start with the campaign, not with understanding the website, brand and customer.

Advertising, performance or visibility are often addressed first. But if the website does not explain the offer, does not feel trustworthy or does not guide people to the next step, advertising only brings more people to the same problem.

Common scenario

  • visitors arrive

  • the result is expensive chaos

Too few inquiries

When the website exists but does not bring enough contacts.

The audit distinguishes whether the problem lies in an unclear offer, weak CTA, lack of trust, poor mobile journey or the fact that the visitor does not know why to continue.

  • finds places where the decision gets lost,
  • names barriers in the main user journey,
  • recommends priorities for increasing clarity and trust.
Weak marketing results

Before increasing the budget, check the website.

If advertising brings people to a website that does not explain value, does not feel trustworthy or does not lead to the next step, performance is lost before contact.

  • the audit shows whether the website can persuade the visitor,
  • reveals weak points in communication and UX,
  • helps decide what to fix before the next campaign.
Redesign

So the new website is not just a prettier version of the same problem.

The audit helps create a redesign brief based on reality: what does not work, what to keep, what to change and what communication role the new website should take.

  • prioritizes content, structure, CTA and trust,
  • names the brand archetype and communication tone,
  • helps give the agency or designer a clearer input.
Unclear offer

A good offer is not enough if the website cannot say it.

Sigel helps clarify what the company actually offers, who it is for, why people should believe it and how to adapt the message to different consumer archetypes.

  • reveals generic or blurry wording,
  • suggests clearer arguments and proof elements,
  • connects the offer with customer motivations.
Stagnation

When the company has grown, but the website stayed in the previous version.

The audit checks whether the website still matches the current offer, audience, price level, trust and the way the company wants to be perceived in the market.

  • shows mismatch between company reality and the website,
  • names communication and strategic weaknesses,
  • sets priorities for what to change first.
